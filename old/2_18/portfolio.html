DC/DC Converter Design

To accompany a undergraduate course on power electronics I made a 100 mW boost converter. My goal for the project was to create an inexpensive and small converter that could be used to power a small motor, or any other hobbyist project.

The converter took a 5 to 9 Volt input and produced a 12 to 50 Volt output. The level was precisely adjusted using a potentiometer and small screwdriver in a set it and forget it fashion. Including all components, the converter cost about $12 which met my objective.

The project gave me tons of practical insight into the non-ideal characteristics of circuit elements. Additionally, the time spent simulating the subcircuits became incredibly useful throughout my education.
